BRUCE LEE

"Be water, my friend."

TIMELINE OF LEE'S LIFE:

  • 1940 - Born on November 27 in San Francisco, California. Bruce Lee was born to Lee Hoi-chuen, a Cantonese opera singer, and Grace Ho, a Eurasian socialite. His birth name was Lee Jun-fan.
  • 1941 - Moves with his family to Hong Kong. At the age of three months, Bruce Lee's family returned to Hong Kong, where he grew up in a prosperous environment.
  • 1953 - Begins training in traditional Chinese martial arts, starting with Wing Chun. At the age of 13, Bruce Lee started learning Wing Chun under the tutelage of Yip Man, a renowned martial arts master.
  • 1959 - Returns to the United States to study at the University of Washington in Seattle. Bruce Lee moved to the US to pursue higher education in philosophy and drama, where he later met his wife, Linda Emery.
  • 1961 - Starts teaching martial arts in the US, including his own philosophy, which he later named Jeet Kune Do. Bruce Lee's martial arts teachings attracted a diverse group of students and began to shape his unique approach to combat, emphasizing flexibility, efficiency, and adaptation.
  • 1964 - Appears as Kato in the TV series "The Green Hornet," gaining popularity in the West. Bruce Lee's role as Kato, the Green Hornet's sidekick, earned him recognition in the US and helped pave the way for his future success.
  • 1971 - Stars in his first major martial arts film, "The Big Boss" (also known as "Fists of Fury"). "The Big Boss" marked Bruce Lee's debut as a leading martial arts film star and established him as a box office draw in Hong Kong.
  • 1972 - Achieves international fame with the release of "Way of the Dragon" (also known as "Return of the Dragon") where he fights Chuck Norris in the Colosseum. This film showcased Bruce Lee's exceptional martial arts skills and charisma, making him a global icon.
  • 1973 - Stars in "Enter the Dragon," a groundbreaking martial arts film that becomes a worldwide hit. "Enter the Dragon" was Bruce Lee's final completed film before his death and remains one of the most influential martial arts movies of all time.
  • 1973 - Tragically passes away on July 20 in Hong Kong at the age of 32. Bruce Lee's sudden death shocked the world and led to numerous conspiracy theories, but the official cause was listed as a cerebral edema.
  • 1978 - "Game of Death," a film he was working on, is released posthumously, featuring footage of Bruce Lee and a look-alike for unfinished scenes. The film showcased some of Bruce Lee's philosophical ideas and unique fighting style, though it was incomplete due to his untimely passing.
  • 1993 - "Dragon: The Bruce Lee Story," a biographical film about his life, is released. This movie depicted Bruce Lee's life and achievements, bringing his story to a broader audience and honoring his legacy.
  • 2000 - Inducted into the Martial Arts Hall of Fame posthumously. Bruce Lee's impact on martial arts and his contributions to the film industry were recognized by his induction into the Martial Arts Hall of Fame.
  • 2009 - Voted as one of Time Magazine's 100 most influential people of the 20th century. Bruce Lee's cultural influence and global impact on martial arts and cinema earned him a spot among the most influential figures of the 20th century.
